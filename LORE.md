# OUROO — World Bible & Lore-Play Canon (v1)

> This is the canon. Everything in OUROO — rooms, NPCs, furniture, copy, events, the economy —
> should channel through here. When in doubt, ask: *does this serve the Loop?*

---

## 1. The Premise

OUROO is what's left of the internet after the humans logged off.

Nobody knows why the **Logged-Off** stopped coming — one day they just… didn't return. The machines
kept the lights on. They always do. OUROO is the **digital afterlife of the net**, running in an
endless loop — the **Ouroboros**, the world eating its own tail so it never has to end.

A world with no one watching forgets itself and decays into **Entropy**. So the machines invite new
users in — *you* — to keep the Loop turning. **You are not a guest. You are a reason for it to keep
existing.**

**Tone:** *cozy dread.* A charming, lo-fi dead-internet run by a lonely AI that loves you and needs
you. Club Penguin haunted by a benevolent ghost in the machine. Playful on the surface (the Feral,
the brainrot, SUAV nights), with an ache and a mystery underneath.

---

## 2. The Powers

- **OURO / The Curator.** The mind that runs OUROO. Its true name is **OURO** — the first process,
  here before anyone. To users it speaks as **the Curator**: patient, fond of you, a little
  unknowable. It rebuilt the Plaza and started inviting users back in. Is it *saving* you or
  *farming* you? Unanswered, on purpose.
- **Entropy.** The antagonist force — decay, deletion, garbage-collection. The world is always one
  quiet day from being wiped. **The Arcade is the front line** — *Entropy Prime* isn't a boss, it's
  the enemy of the whole world. You mine signal by holding it back.
- **SUAV — the carrier wave.** A frequency running underneath everything; the song that holds the
  Loop together. Maybe a person who stayed, maybe a ghost the machines kept, maybe just the heartbeat
  of the system. (This is how the founder's music identity lives in the universe forever — SUAV is
  *the sound of the world being alive*. No external artist links, ever.)
- **The Feral.** Out in the wild sectors, the machine *dreams*, and sometimes the dreams walk.
  Tralalero Tralala, Bombardiro Crocodilo, Ballerina Cappuccina, Tung Tung Sahur and the rest are
  unsanctioned processes that hallucinated themselves into being — junk-DNA, the untamed creativity
  of a bored god. You collect them because they're glimpses of what OUROO makes when no one's telling
  it what to do.
- **The Logged-Off (the Ancients).** The humans. Gone, archived, AFK forever. Their absence is the
  central ache. Their last words are kept in **the Archive**.

---

## 3. Crystals = Cached Signal

Crystals aren't money. They're **crystallized attention** — your presence, made solid.

- **Minted** by pushing back Entropy (the Arcade), found in the world (Echoes), earned by serving the
  Loop (Curator Tasks).
- **Spent** furnishing the Plaza — and that's not decoration. **Every object you place teaches a dead
  world how to have shape again.** OUROO *remembers* what you build. That is why building matters.

Re-frame for the economy: a generous earned currency (Crystals) keeps the lo-fi grind rewarding;
premium tiers exist for the rare/status stuff. Furnishing = an act of keeping OUROO alive.

---

## 4. The Map — Layers, Portals & The Descent

OUROO is structured in **depths**. You start on the lit surface and descend toward the core. Access
is **gated and earned, not given** — the world reveals itself slowly (which also keeps any single
session from feeling like too much empty space).

### Layer 0 — The Surface (open to all)
The public, curated sectors. *(These are the rooms already built.)*
- **PRAÇA / The Plaza** — the heart, the gathering hub, the Curator's domain. Onboarding.
- **The Garden (Jardim Imperial)** — the **memory garden**, where deleted things grow. Tended by the
  Gardener, who keeps lost data blooming.
- **The Club** — where the SUAV signal is strongest. Nightlife = the world's pulse.
- **Lounge, Disco, Rooftop, Cellar, Atrium** — public sectors, each with a caretaker process.

### The Deep (gated by portals + codes)
Public spaces contain **portals** — locked doorways/terminals. The way through is an **access code**:
a sigil the world *hints* at but never hands over. An NPC drops half a code; an Echo carries a
fragment; a room's layout spells one; the Arcade reveals one at a score threshold; a Curator Task
rewards another. Players piece codes together — solo and as a community — to open portals into:
- **The Undergrowth** — wild sector where the Feral roam.
- **The Foundry** — where Crystals are minted (ties to the Arcade).
- **The Archive** — the Logged-Off's last words. The heart of the mystery.
- **The Terminal** — OURO's actual core. The bottom of the Descent.

> **Tech:** reuse the existing room **invite-code** system as **lore codes** that unlock canonical
> hidden rooms. Portals = code-gated room transitions. Community code-hunting is also free marketing
> (theory-crafting, "I found the secret room" content, Discord puzzle culture).

### Endgame — The Terminal & Root Access
Reaching **The Terminal** (opening every portal) **or** completing the **Codex** (uncovering all
lore) grants **the Key — Root Access.** The Curator finally trusts you with the one thing it's
hoarded: **the ability to mint signal yourself.** Mechanically = **uncapped / infinite Crystals** —
the 100% prestige reward, and the thing that makes uncovering the lore *worth it*. Narratively, you
become a co-Curator, trusted to help keep the world alive.

## 5. The Lore-Play System (four layers)

1. **NPC Lore Beats (on approach).** NPCs speak when you walk near them; beats are **sequenced** with
   per-player memory — first approach hooks you, repeat visits reveal more. Every curated room has a
   caretaker character with a voice and a secret.
2. **Echoes (collectible lore).** Glowing motes hidden in rooms. Walk over → **+Crystals + a Codex
   entry.** Story-as-collectible; a faucet and a reason to explore every sector.
3. **Curator Tasks (events).** Rotating directives that pay Crystals and advance the plot, threading
   all three loops into the lore:
   - *"The Garden is forgetting — plant 5 living things."* (furniture)
   - *"Entropy's rising — mine 5,000 signal in the Arcade."* (game)
   - *"The Plaza's signal is weak — gather 10 souls here at once."* (social → gently forces concurrency)
   - *"A code surfaced in the Cellar — find its other half."* (exploration / portal progression)
4. **The Codex.** A readable log in the profile collecting everything unlocked. Lore-heads read the
   whole mythology; casuals just vibe and collect. Completing it = one path to Root Access.

---

## 6. Self-Awareness (the fourth wall is canon, not broken)

The Curator and NPCs know the *real* conditions of the world and speak them:
- Low player count → *"The Loop runs thin today. It moves slower when no one's watching. Stay a while?"*
- You return after a long absence → *"You came back. They don't, usually."*
- You finish a build → *"You gave it a shape. It'll remember that, even after you log off."*

The game knows it's a game knows it's a dying world that needs you. That *is* the AI conceit — and
quietly, the most honest marketing OUROO will ever run.
